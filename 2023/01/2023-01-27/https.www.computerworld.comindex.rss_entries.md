<p>Poor sales of PC and server chips caused Intel’s revenue for the fourth quarter of 2022 to dive 32% year on year, leading to a $664 million net loss for the quarter.</p><p>The outlook for Q1 of 2023 is not an optimistic one either, with CEO Pat Gelsinger telling analysts in a call, “Our results and our Q1 guidance are below what we expect of ourselves.”  </p><p>However, he said that Intel is “working diligently to address the challenges brought on by current demand trends” and the company continues to have confidence in its long-term plans and trajectory.</p><p>Intel took the biggest hits across its two largest business units, with the chip maker’s Client Computing Group (CCG) and Data Center and AI group (DCAI) posting year-on-year revenue drops of 36% and 33%, respectively.</p><p class="jumpTag"><a href="https://www.computerworld.com/article/3686475/intel-suffers-double-digit-revenue-decline-in-q4-2022.html#jump">To read this article in full, please click here</a></p></section></article>
