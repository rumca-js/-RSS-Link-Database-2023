<p>Building on its WebEx product line, Cisco plans to deliver an air-gapped, cloud-based collaboration system  for companies involved in US national security and defense work, extending the secure offerings the company already provides to industries that require collaboration tools with strong security measures to meet US government requirements.</p><p>Beginning in 2024, the new Webex system — Air-Gapped Trusted Cloud — will provide an added layer of security for teams collaborating through the Webex App, Cisco said.</p><p>An air gap is a security measure that involves isolating a computer or network and preventing it from establishing an external connection. For example, an air-gapped computer is unable to connect to the internet or any other communications networks so as to have complete security with the information that resides within it.</p><p class="jumpTag"><a href="https://www.computerworld.com/article/3693449/cisco-to-offer-webex-air-gapped-cloud-system-for-security-defense-work.html#jump">To read this article in full, please click here</a></p></section></article>

## Massachusetts lawmakers file bill to create 4-day workweek pilot
 - [https://www.computerworld.com/article/3692895/massachusetts-lawmakers-file-bill-to-create-4-day-workweek-pilot.html#tk.rss_all](https://www.computerworld.com/article/3692895/massachusetts-lawmakers-file-bill-to-create-4-day-workweek-pilot.html#tk.rss_all)
 - RSS feed: https://www.computerworld.com/index.rss
 - date published: 2023-04-13 10:00:00+00:00

<article>
	<section class="page">
<p>Following a similar program in Europe, two Massachusetts lawmakers have <a href="https://malegislature.gov/Bills/193/HD4267" rel="nofollow">filed a bill</a> this week to create a two-year pilot program for a four-day workweek.</p><p>Rep. Dylan Fernandes of Falmouth and Rep. Josh Cutler of Duxbury filed the bill that would create a voluntary program allowing participating businesses to transition some or all of their employees to a four-day week without loss of pay or benefits. </p><p>The pilot program would run for two years and would be overseen by the Executive Office of Labor and Workforce Development. To participate, employers must agree to transition at least 15 workers to a shortened workweek.</p><p class="jumpTag"><a href="https://www.computerworld.com/article/3692895/massachusetts-lawmakers-file-bill-to-create-4-day-workweek-pilot.html#jump">To read this article in full, please click here</a></p></section></article>
