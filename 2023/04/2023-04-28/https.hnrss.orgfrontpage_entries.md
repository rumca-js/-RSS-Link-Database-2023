## Tell HN: Cloudflare verification is breaking the internet

 - RSS feed: https://hnrss.org/frontpage
 - date published: 2023-04-28 15:38:43+00:00

<p>Across many different pages including science journals, ChatGPT, and many others, CloudFlare verification goes into an infinite loop of:<p>1. "Verify you are a human"<p>2. Check the box or perform some other type of rain dance<p>3. "Please stand by, while we are checking your browser..."<p>4. Repeat step 1<p>I'm on Fedora Linux 37 using Firefox 110.<p>The workaround is to use Chrome.<p>After experiencing this dozens of times and getting annoyed of needing to use Chrome, I finally went and deleted all my cookies and cache which I had been dreading to do.<p>It did not help.<p>I don't have a CloudFlare account so I wrote up a detailed post on their community forums. I offered a HAR file and was willing to do diagnostics. It received no responses and it was auto-closed.<p>It's unacceptable that CloudFlare is breaking the internet while offering no community support.<p>Edit: I'm in Texas. I'm not using a VPN or Tor, just AT&amp;T Fiber. I don't have ad-blockers. No weird extensions. Nothing special (besides being on Linux).<p>Edit2: Since this got traction, I opened a new community post: https://community.cloudflare.com/t/infinite-verify-you-are-a-human-loop/503065<p>To be clear, I'm not against CloudFlare doing DDoS protection, etc., but it can't be breaking the internet <i>while ignoring community posts on it</i>.<p>Edit3: The CloudFlare team has engaged. Thank you HN!</p>
