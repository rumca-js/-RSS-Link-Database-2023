## Ask HN: When LLMs make stuff up, call it 'confabulating', not 'hallucinating'
 - [https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=37912421](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=37912421)
 - RSS feed: https://hnrss.org/frontpage
 - date published: 2023-10-17T09:19:53+00:00

<p>In humans, a hallucination is formally defined as being a sensory experience without an external stimulus. LLMs have no sensors to experience the world with (other than their text input) and (probably) don’t even <i>have</i> a subjective experience in the same way humans do.<p>A more suitable term would be confabulation, which is what humans do when due to a memory error (eg. due to Korsakoff syndrome) we produce distorted memories of oneself. This may sometimes sound very believable to outsiders; the comparison with LLMs making stuff up is rather apt!<p>So please call it confabulating instead of hallucinating when LLMs make stuff up.</p>
