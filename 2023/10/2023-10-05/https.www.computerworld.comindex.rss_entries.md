<p>Taiwan’s Ministry of Economic Affairs has agreed to investigate Taiwanese suppliers in business with Huawei for aiding the operation of its chipmaking factories in China.</p><p>The investigation, which was first reported by <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2023-10-05/taiwan-to-probe-suppliers-helping-huawei-with-china-chip-plants" rel="nofollow">Bloomberg</a>, would determine if these suppliers have violated any US sanctions, Economic Affairs Minister Wang Mei-hua <a href="https://ivod.ly.gov.tw/Play/Clip/1M/147274" rel="nofollow">told the country’s legislative body</a>.</p><p>The investigation, which will cover at least four Taiwanese firms, will probe whether these companies are operating within their scope as specified in their application to the Taiwanese government.</p><p class="jumpTag"><a href="/article/3708268/taiwan-to-investigate-suppliers-aiding-huawei-in-chipmaking-report.html#jump">To read this article in full, please
