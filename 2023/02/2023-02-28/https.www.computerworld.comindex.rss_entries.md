 - RSS feed: https://www.computerworld.com/index.rss
 - date published: 2023-02-28 16:12:00+00:00

<article>
	<section class="page">
<p>Videoconferencing company Zoom is pinning its growth plans on investment in AI, after reporting its first fourth quarter net loss since 2018.</p><p>Reporting <a href="https://investors.zoom.us/news-releases/news-release-details/zoom-video-communications-reports-fourth-quarter-and-fiscal-2" rel="nofollow">results</a> on Monday, Zoom said it posted a net loss of $104 million for the fourth quarter of its fiscal 2023, ended January 31, compared to a profit of $490 million in the year-earlier period. Online revenue was also down 10% year over year to $481.7 million.</p><p>The loss was largely a result of stock-based compensation expenses. Otherwise, Zoom recorded fourth quarter total revenue of $1.1 billion, up 4% year over year, with total revenue for the fiscal year 2022 reported as $4.4 billion, up 7% year over year.</p><p class="jumpTag"><a href="https://www.computerworld.com/article/3688952/zoom-sets-sights-on-ai-development-as-it-posts-fourth-quarter-loss.html#jump">To read this article in full, please click here</a></p></section></article>
