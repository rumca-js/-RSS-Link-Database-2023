## Nintendo confirms it won't be at E3 2023
 - [https://www.engadget.com/nintendo-confirms-it-wont-be-at-e3-2023-184336696.html?src=rss](https://www.engadget.com/nintendo-confirms-it-wont-be-at-e3-2023-184336696.html?src=rss)
 - RSS feed: https://www.engadget.com/rss.xml
 - date published: 2023-02-24 18:43:36+00:00

<p><a href="https://www.engadget.com/tag/nintendo/"><ins>Nintendo</ins></a> has decided not to take part in this year's <a href="https://www.engadget.com/tag/e3/"><ins>E3</ins></a>. &quot;We approach our involvement in any event on a case-by-case basis and are always considering various ways to engage with our fans,&quot; Nintendo told Engadget in a statement. &quot;Since this year’s E3 show didn’t fit into our plans, we have made the decision to not participate. However, we have been and continue to be a strong supporter of the ESA [Entertainment Software Association] and E3.”</p><p>Nintendo typically has a presence on the E3 showfloor and it has often run Treehouse broadcasts throughout the event in addition to a Direct. The company took part in the last proper edition of E3 in 2019, as well as the <a href="https://www.engadget.com/e3-2021-schedule-streams-what-expect-160054773.html"><ins>all-digital affair in 2021</ins></a>. There was no E3 in <a href="https://www.engadget.com/2020-03-11-e3-2020-is-officially-cancelled.html"><ins>2020</ins></a> or <a href="https://www.engadget.com/e3-2022-fully-canceled-194216531.html"><ins>2022</ins></a>.</p><span id="end-legacy-contents"></span><p>Earlier this month, <a href="https://www.ign.com/articles/xbox-nintendo-sony-skipping-e3-2023"><em><ins>IGN </ins></em><ins>reported</ins></a> that Nintendo, Microsoft and Sony have all bowed out of E3 this year. Sony hasn't had a presence at the event <a href="https://www.engadget.com/2018-11-15-sony-e3-2019-playstation.html"><ins>since 2018</ins></a>, but neither it nor Microsoft have publicly confirmed plans for this year's edition. Regardless, Microsoft will host its <a href="https://news.xbox.com/en-us/2023/01/25/xbox-bethesda-developer-direct-everything-announced-recap/"><ins>big annual showcase</ins></a> in Los Angeles this summer. Microsoft Gaming CEO Phil Spencer recently <a href="https://www.ign.com/articles/phil-spencer-interview-2023"><ins>hinted</ins></a> it will take place around the same time as E3, which will run between June 13th and 16th.</p><p>There could always be a Nintendo Direct in mid-June as well, even if it's not officially connected to the expo. Meanwhile, <a href="https://www.pushsquare.com/news/2023/02/rumour-sony-saving-ps5s-good-stuff-for-ps-showcase-prior-to-e3-2023"><ins>word on the street</ins></a> is that PlayStation is planning a major showcase ahead of E3 after this week's <a href="https://www.engadget.com/humanity-was-the-most-interesting-game-at-sonys-state-of-play-and-you-can-play-a-demo-today-215456616.html"><ins>so-so State of Play stream</ins></a>.</p><p>The absence of just one of the big three console manufacturers from E3 is a blow for the ESA, let alone all of them. Summer Game Fest is threatening to steal more of the stalwart event's thunder too. But hey, at least <a href="https://www.gamesindustry.biz/ubisoft-confirmed-for-e3-2023"><ins>Ubisoft will be at E3</ins></a>.</p>

## Scientists find evidence of a new layer at the Earth's inner core
 - [https://www.engadget.com/earth-new-layer-innermost-inner-core-discovery-170411286.html?src=rss](https://www.engadget.com/earth-new-layer-innermost-inner-core-discovery-170411286.html?src=rss)
 - RSS feed: https://www.engadget.com/rss.xml
 - date published: 2023-02-24 17:04:11+00:00

<p>Researchers are still discovering more about <a href="https://www.engadget.com/2017-04-09-japan-chikyu-drilling-ship-earth-mantle.html">the Earth's center</a>. A team at Australian National University (ANU) has <a href="https://www.anu.edu.au/news/all-news/bouncing-seismic-waves-reveal-distinct-layer-in-earths-core">found evidence</a> of a new layer to the planet sitting within the inner core. This &quot;innermost inner core&quot; is an iron-nickel alloy ball that, as professor Hrvoje Tkalčić explains, is a &quot;fossilized record&quot; of Earth's ancient history. Until now, science had only recognized four layers (crust, mantle, outer core and inner core).</p><p>The scientists found the 'hidden' core by studying seismic waves that traveled back and forth across the Earth's entire diameter up to five times — previous studies only looked at single bounces. The earthquake waves probed places near the center at angles that suggested a different crystalline structure inside the innermost layer. Effectively, the alloy is skewing the travel times for the waves as they pass through.</p><span id="end-legacy-contents"></span><p>The findings open up new ways to investigate the inner core, according to lead author Thanh-Son Phạm. ANU also believes the innermost inner core hints at a major event in Earth's past that had a &quot;significant&quot; impact on the planet's heart. As researchers <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/climate-environment/2023/02/24/new-earth-inner-core-layer-metallic-ball/">explain</a> to <em>The Washington Post</em>, it could also help explain the formation of the Earth's magnetic field. The field plays a major role in supporting life as it shields the Earth from harmful radiation and keeps water from drifting into space.</p><p>Those insights may help with studies of other worlds. Mars is believed to be a barren planet because it <a href="https://www.engadget.com/2019-05-06-dust-storms-ate-water-on-mars.html">lost its magnetic field</a> roughly four billion years ago, leaving no protection against solar winds and dust storms that carried away the atmosphere and oceans. Exoplanet hunters, meanwhile, could use the knowledge to search for <a href="https://www.engadget.com/2017-04-23-most-habitable-planets-totally-covered-in-water.html">habitable worlds</a>. The presence of an Earth-like core structure isn't guaranteed to indicate survivability, but may play a role in narrowing down candidate planets.</p>
