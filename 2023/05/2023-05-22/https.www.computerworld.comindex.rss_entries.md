	<section class="page">
<p>The fax — that 1940s technology that exploded in the 1980s and operates by copying an image and transmitting it through squeaks and squawks over a phone line — is still used by a large majority of healthcare providers, insurance payers, and pharmacies.</p><p>And it’s simply not going away anytime soon.</p><p>As recently as 2019, seven in 10 hospitals were still relying on fax machines and phone lines to transfer and retrieve patient records or order prescriptions, according to the <a href="https://www.healthit.gov/sites/default/files/page/2021-03/Hospital%20Use%20of%20Certified%20HIT_Interop%20v10_1.pdf" rel="nofollow noopener" target="_blank">latest figures from the Office of the National Coordinator for Health Information Technology</a> (ONC).
